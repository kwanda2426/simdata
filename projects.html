Continuum Mechanics




    CFD is one of the most reliable tool used to solve complex engineering systems. Whether one is interested in solving a wide variety of engineering problems such as optimization of the reactor performance, predicting carbon emissions, developing medical devices, predicting vehicle aerodynamics. CFD can be put to use to gain increase knowledge on how system components are expected to operate so as to make required improvements for design and optimization studies.

    Several CFD Methods available in solving complex engineering problems involve:

    - General purpose Navier Stokes Solvers used to simulate thermal and other general fluid flow applications.

    - Smoothed particle hydrodynamics Solvers used to simulate fluid mixing, fluid sloshing and free surface flows.

    - Lattice Boltzmann Method Solvers used to simulate transient aerodynamics and aeroacoustics problems.

    - These CFD solutions are readily available as in opensource codes which the general research community and industrial practioners can utilise to address a wide range of application to minimize the costs of software licenses that comes with commercial software codes.



    Discrete Element Method




    DEM is a numerical computational tool used to simulate the motion and interaction of a large number of particles such as granular matter and bulk materials.

    Typical DEM Solution methods used to solve particulate engineering problems include:


    • Thermal DEM Models used to calculate thermal properties of particles via modeling conductive and convective heat transfer.

    • Particle Breakage Models used to predict complex particle breakage by taking into account material properties and dynamic collision energetics.

    • Complex Particle Motion and Particle Shapes used to account for complicated particle motion and as well as customised 3D particles shapes.
